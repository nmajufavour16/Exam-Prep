Topic 01

Introduction to IT Entrepreneurship

What is Entrepreneurship?

Definition Entrepreneurship is the process of identifying opportunities, taking risks and organising resources to create new ventures. It drives technological advancement and economic growth. IT Entrepreneurship specifically focuses on using technology and digital innovation to build businesses.
  • Entrepreneurship brings about technological advancement
  • It promotes digital inclusive initiatives
  • It supports environmentally friendly technology
  • It helps develop usable and sustainable technology
  • It involves the disposal and management of electronic waste
  • It requires adherence to legal and ethical compliance

Characteristics of Entrepreneurship

1. Vision & Goal Oriented

Entrepreneurs have a clear vision of what they want to achieve and set specific goals to get there.

2. Problem-Solving Skill

They identify problems in the market and develop solutions through products or services.

3. Persistence & Resilience

They persist through failure and bounce back — resilience is a core entrepreneurial trait.

4. Good Leadership Ability

They can inspire, motivate and lead teams toward a shared vision.

5. Creative and Innovative

They constantly generate new ideas and find better ways to solve problems.

Intrapreneurship

Definition Intrapreneurship involves the entrepreneurial activities carried out within an existing organisation by the employees — existing within an organisation. Employees act like entrepreneurs inside the company to drive innovation.

Equity Capital

Topic 05

Legal Business Formation & Registration

Types of Business Formation

Sole Proprietorship

Owned and run by one person. Simplest form. Owner has full control but is personally liable for all debts.

Partnership

Two or more people share ownership. Partners share profits, losses and liability. Governed by a partnership agreement.

Limited (Private) Company

Separate legal entity from its owners (shareholders). Limited liability — owners only lose what they invested. Most common for IT startups.

Foundation / Non-profit

Established not for profit but for a social, educational or charitable mission. Surplus reinvested into the mission.

Business Registration Process

CAC — Corporate Affairs Commission Every IT venture must be legally registered under an appropriate regulatory agency — in Nigeria, the CAC (Corporate Affairs Commission). Legal registration ensures recognition as a legal entity.
  • Register the business with the CAC
  • Obtain a Business Identification Number (BIN)
  • Begin the identification/foundation process
  • Registration gives the ability to enter contracts, access funding and banking services
  • Also provides legal protection and legitimacy

Business Regulations & Licensing

Why Registration Matters Every IT venture must be legally required under the appropriate regulatory agency (i.e. CAC). Legal registration ensures recognition as a legal entity. It also ensures the ability to enter into contracts, access funding and banking services, and receive legal protections.
Topic 06

Intellectual Property & Legal Regulations

Intellectual Property (IP) — Four Types

a) Copyright

Protects software code, digital content, websites and databases. Ensures unauthorized persons cannot use your material without permission.

b) Patent

Protects inventions and technology innovations from theft. Gives the inventor exclusive rights to use, sell or license the invention for a set period.

c) Trademark

Protects brand names, logos and symbols. Ensures no one else can use your brand identity in commerce.

d) Trade Secret

Protects confidential business information and trade processes from competitors. E.g. Coca-Cola's formula. Unauthorized copying and revenue losses are prevented.

Data Protection and Privacy Laws

Data Protection IT businesses collect and process customer data — laws regulate how such data must be handled. It ensures that any data collected comes with consent. Failure to abide by these laws may lead to fines, legal actions and reputational damage.

Cybercrime and Fraud Regulations

Cybercrime Laws Cybercrime addresses laws such as hacking, identity theft, online fraud and financial crimes. IT entrepreneurship must ensure that their system is secured and comply with cybersecurity regulations.

Consumer Protection Laws

  • These laws protect consumers from false advertising
  • Misleading information about products/services
  • Unfair pricing and deceptive business practices
  • IT businesses must be transparent about what their product does and does not do

Product Development and Innovation Process

Key Definitions

Product Innovation A process of creating and introducing new and improved products in the market, or improving already existing products.
Product Development The process of taking a product from the idea created to the market.

StageNameDetails

Role of Technology in Product Development

  • Technology facilitates rapid development
  • Technology can be cross-applied to experts in related fields
  • Existing products can be redesigned using technology to add new value
  • Technology enables terms in ideation and federal development

User-Centred Design (UCD)

User-Centred Design Focuses on attaining users' needs and experience. The five steps are: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Execute (test).
StepAction
EmpathizeUnderstand the user's problems, needs and context deeply
DefineClearly define the problem statement based on research
IdeateGenerate multiple creative solutions
PrototypeBuild a basic model/mockup of the solution
Execute / TestTest prototype with real users; gather feedback

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

MVP Has to do with bringing a small sample of a product to test assumptions and gather feedback and findings. It includes product testing and improvement. MVP is about validating ideas with minimum effort before full development.

Three Categories of MVP Testing

1. User Testing

Testing the product with real target users to assess usability and satisfaction.

2. Beta Testing

Software testing where a nearly finished product is released to the community outside the development team to gather real-world feedback.

3. Performance Testing

Testing how the product performs under expected load, stress and speed conditions.

Stage VIII — Product Development and Testing

Stage VIII Involves control and uses the tendencies to make improvements where needed. It helps reduce the risk of failure. It uses a cost/beta test approach — entails monitoring performance, improving the production process.

Stage IX — Market Extension and Expansion

Stage IX Entails monitoring performance, improving the production, and expanding into new markets. It involves externalising the product and listening to market feedback to serve aligned customers and fill value gaps still present.

Legal Issues and Business Ethics in IT Entrepreneurship

Overview

Context Entrepreneurship operates within a legal and ethical environment that regulates business activities and protects stakeholders. As a digital business expert globally, compliance with legal standards and adherence to ethical principles have become critical for sustainability and reputation growth.

Concept of Business Ethics

Business Ethics Business ethics refers to the moral principles and standards that guide the behaviours in business. In IT Entrepreneurship, ethics refers to the responsible use of technology, fair dealings with customers, protection of user data and transparency in digital transactions.

5 Principles of Business Ethics

#PrincipleMeaning
1IntegrityBeing honest in all business dealings — say what you mean and do what you say
2AccountabilityTaking responsibility for your actions and decisions
3TransparencyOpenness — void of secrecy. Being clear about how your business operates
4Respect for PrivacyProtecting user data and not misusing personal information
5Social ResponsibilityContributing positively to society beyond just making profit

Legal Issues in IT Entrepreneurship

Legal issues refer to laws and regulations governing business activities in the digital environment. Key areas include:

  • Data Protection and Privacy Laws — regulate how user data is collected and processed
  • Cybercrime and Fraud Regulations — hacking, identity theft, online fraud, financial crimes
  • Consumer Protection Laws — false advertising, misleading information
  • Intellectual Property Laws — copyright, patent, trademark, trade secret
  • Business Regulations & Licensing — CAC registration, compliance

Ethical Issues in IT Entrepreneurship

Ethical Challenges IT Entrepreneurs Face Beyond legal compliance in IT, they face several ethical issues including: data privacy and surveillance; collecting excessive user data or misusing customer information is unethical; quality lag in digital services; and transparency in digital marketing.

What Entrepreneurs Must Avoid

Fake Reviews

Creating or paying for fake positive reviews of your product is deceptive and unethical.

Clickbait Advertising

Using sensational, exaggerated or misleading headlines to attract attention. Violates transparency principles.

Software Piracy & Plagiarism

Using unlicensed software or copying code without permission. A legal and ethical violation.

Excessive Data Collection

Collecting more user data than necessary or misusing customer information for profit is unethical.

Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)

CSR Corporate Social Responsibility refers to a company's social obligation to contribute positively to the society. It is a company's social obligation to contribute to society beyond its core business activities — e.g. environmental initiatives, community development.

Transparency in Digital Marketing

Entrepreneurs must be transparent in digital marketing. This means: clearly stating what product/service does, being honest about pricing, not using manipulative advertising techniques, and disclosing sponsored content and partnerships.

Topic 09

Business Plan Development

Definition

Business Plan A Business Plan is a written document that outlines the ideas, strategy and financial projections of a company. It addresses the business's development, the policies, components and how the business will be run.

Feasibility Analysis (Before the Business Plan)

Market Feasibility

Is there a market for this product? Who are the customers? Is the market large enough?

Technical Feasibility

Has to do with the technology aspect — can the technology required actually be built or acquired?

Financial Feasibility

Do we have enough capital? Can the business generate profit? What is the break-even point?

Legal Feasibility

Are there any legal barriers? Can the business be legally registered and operated?

Components of a Business Plan

ComponentDescription
a) Company DescriptionWho you are, what you do, your mission, vision and values
b) Market AnalysisTarget market, market size, competitors, market trends
c) Product DescriptionWhat product/service you offer, its features and unique value
d) Operational PlanHow the business will run day-to-day — processes, location, team
e) Management StructureOrganisational chart — who runs what, roles and responsibilities
f) FinanceStartup costs, revenue projections, profit/loss forecast, funding needs

Reasons for Business Plan Development

  • It attracts investors — demonstrates seriousness and potential
  • It secures a bank loan — banks require a business plan before lending
  • It guides the business operations — a roadmap for decisions
  • It reduces risk — forces you to think through problems before they occur
  • It brings about economic development
  • It brings about innovation

Q24 — Beta TestingWhat is Beta Testing? How does it differ from User Testing and Performance Testing?
Beta Testing: Software testing where a nearly finished product is released to the community outside the development team to gather real-world feedback before the final launch.

Three MVP Testing Types Compared:
  • User Testing — Controlled testing with selected target users to assess usability and satisfaction. Done INSIDE controlled environment.
  • Beta Testing — Released to external community; real-world, uncontrolled. Reveals bugs and issues developers didn't anticipate. Done OUTSIDE the development team.
  • Performance Testing — Tests how the system behaves under load, stress and speed conditions. Focuses on technical performance, not user experience.
Q25 — SummaryWhat is the relationship between: Creativity, Innovation, Entrepreneurship, Product Development, Business Plan, Legal Formation, and Market Expansion?
These concepts form a connected entrepreneurial lifecycle:

Creativity generates ideas → Innovation turns ideas into viable solutions → Entrepreneurship organises resources and takes risks to build a venture around those solutions → Product Development translates the venture idea into a tangible product (through Idea Generation, Screening, Development, Testing) → The Business Plan documents the strategy, financial projections and operations → Legal Formation (CAC registration, IP protection, compliance) protects and legitimises the business → MVP and Testing validate the product in the market → Market Expansion (Stage IX) grows the validated product to new customers and markets.

Each stage feeds into the next — skipping one creates gaps that expose the business to avoidable risks.
